Code out Violence: A Datathon for Crime Analysts

October 25th - 27th, 2019

Hosted by the University of Chicago Crime Lab and Narrative Science




Set on the margins of the upcoming International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP) Annual Conference in Chicago, this event will bring together civilian crime analysts from police agencies across the country-and even the world-for three days of disruptive innovation and collaborative problem-solving. Recognizing the toll of violent crime and illegal firearm use in communities across the country, Code out Violence will spotlight actionable solutions to gun violence.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)


Q: What is a Datathon?

A Datathon is a product-oriented collaborative event designed to help crime analysts learn new ways to integrate data analysis into their work. Analysts will collaborate with other leading members of their field to create actionable intelligence products using cutting-edge technologies. It is an opportunity to develop new skills, expand the crime analyst professional network, and foster some friendly competition.

Q: Who is running Code out Violence?

University of Chicago Crime Lab

Q: When & Where?

When: October 25th to 27th

Where: Narrative Science, 1 N Dearborn St #700, Chicago, IL 60602

Q: Who can attend?

Code out Violence is open to civilian crime analysts from police agencies across the country and even the world.

Q: When is the registration deadline?

Registration will be closing soon, please apply as soon as you can as spots are filling up!

Q: How many analysts will attend?

We ask that departments limit their attendance to one analyst due to space and team-size constraints.

Q: Does it cost anything to participate?

Not only is participation absolutely free; we will be providing breakfast, lunch and snacks along the way. However, lodging and travel to and from the venue expenses are not covered by Code out Violence.

Q: Is there reimbursement for travel?

Thanks to our sponsors, we are able to offer a travel stipend to a limited number of participants.

Q: Is the hackathon related to the International Association of Chiefs of Police (IACP) Annual Conference?

While the hackathon is deliberately scheduled on the margins of the IACP’s upcoming Annual Conference, the two events are separate.

Q: What equipment should I bring?

A laptop and charger are a must, devices or portable equipment you use at work, and anything you need to feel comfortable throughout the event.

Q: Are there any required skill levels?

Any and all civilian analysts are welcomed to participate! While strong analytical skills are a huge plus, each team will need people with presentation skills and brilliant ideas.

Q: How will teams be made?

Each team will consist of anywhere between 5 to 6 analysts, assigned based on a pre-event survey of self-reported skills and areas of interest.

Q: Are there prizes?

Yes! Winning teams will be selected by a panel of judges at the end of the event and awarded a series of prizes and swag by category, courtesy of our sponsors.
